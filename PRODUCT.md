# Product

## Register

brand

The storefront is the primary surface — expressive, identity-driven, art-led (Displate-class). Admin console, checkout, and order flows are treated as **product** register per-task: they serve the workflow and stay quiet.

## Users

- **Art buyers in India** — design-conscious urban customers (and fandom buyers: anime, pop culture) browsing on mobile as often as desktop, shopping for something that makes a wall theirs. They can buy as guests; an account is only needed for order history.
- **Indian digital artists** — curated, manually onboarded via an intake form. They want a dignified stage: attribution, a real artist page, being "celebrated, not processed."
- **Wallmeri admins** — a small team managing the entire catalog, artist pipeline, orders, and review moderation through `/admin`. They need speed and clarity, not brand theatre.

## Product Purpose

Wallmeri is a curated, India-only marketplace for premium metal wall art (sublimation print on stainless steel, magnetic mounting). It exists to put Indian artists' work on Indian walls — "Every Artist Deserves a Wall." Success looks like: buyers trust the quality enough to pay premium prices, artists feel showcased rather than commoditised, and the catalog stays curated (no self-serve upload, no algorithmic feed).

## Brand Personality

**Premium. Assured. Gallery-calm.**

The energy comes from the art, not the chrome. Voice is confident and warm but never shouty — short declarative sentences, quiet swagger, zero discount-speak. The interface behaves like a well-lit gallery: generous space, art as the hero, restrained everything else. Artist-first and Bengaluru-born ("We're small. We're real.") — human, not corporate.

**Open visual question (capture, don't resolve yet):** the About Us deck is full noir — near-black surfaces, bold red accents, art glowing as in a dim gallery. The implemented site is the inverse: light Cotton (#FAF9F6) canvas with Noir Black used as a dramatic frame (header, hero, footer). Both are legitimate readings of the brand. The default today is **light gallery, dark frame**; a shift toward noir is on the table and should be explored deliberately (e.g. via `/impeccable critique` or `live`), not drifted into.

## Anti-references

- **Generic marketplace (Amazon / Flipkart):** dense commodity grids, badge clutter, discount-screaming UI, urgency banners. Wallmeri never begs.
- **SaaS-clean minimalism:** sterile startup-landing grammar — gradient heroes, identical feature cards, no soul.
- **Craft/artisan cliché (Etsy-ish):** handmade-rustic, twee, kraft-paper vibes. Wallmeri is steel — modern, precise, permanent.

## Design Principles

1. **The art is the hero.** Merchandising is art-first: big artwork-led grids, chrome that recedes, no UI element competing with a poster.
2. **Celebrate the artist.** Attribution and artist identity are woven through the shopping flow (cards, product pages, artist pages) — a spotlight, not a byline.
3. **Premium is calm.** Confidence through restraint: space, typography, and material quality signals instead of badges, banners, and noise.
4. **Steel, not paper.** The permanence and precision of the material should be felt in the interface — crisp edges, deliberate motion, nothing flimsy or decorative.
5. **Two registers, one brand.** Storefront surfaces express; admin and checkout surfaces serve. Checkout especially: brand-warm but friction-free — trust is the conversion lever.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA**: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast (≥3:1 large text), full keyboard navigability, visible focus states (already established: 2px Premium Red #B32624 focus-visible outline).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation.
- India-first pragmatics: mobile-heavy audience — touch targets ≥44px, layouts tested at small widths, art imagery optimised so pages stay usable on mid-range devices.
